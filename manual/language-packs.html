Languages

Five languages — each built for a different purpose.

English

For advanced learners and native speakers expanding their vocabulary.

Level packs carry learners from intermediate vocabulary up into advanced territory. The Roots series approaches English through its three etymological strata — Germanic, Norman-French, and Latin — making visible the historical doublets that give English its register system.

Levels:Adept · Advance · Exceptional   ·   Roots:Germanic · Norman · Latin

German

Built for education and business.

Vocabulary for academic writing, professional correspondence, and institutional life. Built for learners working, studying, or settling in the German-speaking world.

Packs:Core · Pareto 1 · Pareto 2

Spanish

Subtitle corpora and academic frequency — everyday comprehension.

Vocabulary drawn from the Spanish people actually encounter: television, film, podcasts, and conversation. Built to make Spanish-language media accessible without subtitles.

Packs:Core · Pareto 1 · Pareto 2

Italian

Cultural heritage and travel.

Regional cuisine, artistic and historical context, and the practical vocabulary of moving through Italian life. Built for travellers, new residents, and anyone drawn to Italy.

Packs:Core · Pareto 1 · Pareto 2

Esperanto

The global Esperantist community.

Esperanto as it's actually used: diplomatic register, international community forums, translated publications, and the worldwide Esperantist movement. Built for learners who want to participate in a living, globally distributed speaker community.

Packs:Core · Pareto 1 · Pareto 2
