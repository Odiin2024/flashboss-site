Esperanto was designed from the ground up to be easy to learn. No irregular verbs. No gendered nouns. Logical, consistent, and beautiful. It's the perfect first language — and if you already speak a second, it might be your fastest path to a third.

FlashBoss starts you with 1,000 of the most useful Esperanto words, each with full audio, a real example sentence, and spaced repetition that bends to how fast you actually learn — that's the free Core pack. Words sit in themed clusters, each one guarded by a boss fight. Win, and those cards graduate out of your daily deck for good. The work shrinks as you learn, not the other way around.

And unlike most invented languages, Esperanto is genuinely spoken. Estimates put the community somewhere between a hundred thousand and two million people scattered across the world, including a thousand or more children raised with it from birth. They gather every summer at the Universala Kongreso — the World Esperanto Congress, held almost without a break since 1905, with the 2026 meeting in Graz, Austria. Online it's easy to fall in with: free courses and a busy forum at lernu.net, over a million learners through the Duolingo course, the Amikumu app for finding speakers near you, and Pasporta Servo, a hospitality network where Esperantists host one another around the world for free. Learn it, and you don't just have a hobby — you have somewhere to use it.
