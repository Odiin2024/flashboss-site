Why I Made This

A language teacher's obsession

The Engine

I'm a CELTA-certified English teacher and an active German learner. I downloaded a spaced repetition program and discovered it was random. So I learned how real spaced repetition programs actually work. Ease factor! I realised the Fibonacci sequence could control interval calculation. It seemed like a better solution.

I built each flashcard with progressively graded language, example sentences using natural German speech patterns. Built for comprehension. It worked beautifully.

CELTA-Certified Language Teacher

Cambridge Certificate in English Language Teaching to Adults

The Interface

When I tried to show it to anybody, my scrolling text running up the page made no sense to them. Then I learned how to flush the screen, and suddenly I had a picture book structure. Choose your own adventure.

I needed to purge cards to make it progressive, and that opened the door to boss fights. Beat a cluster, those cards leave your rotation. Not gamification slapped on top - actual victories. From there, everything fell into place.

Then Piper dropped from the heavens - neural text-to-speech that actually sounds human. Suddenly the whole thing could speak to you.

The Build

I can't code in the traditional sense. FlashBoss was built through conversation - me describing what I needed, AI helping me build it. Every feature exists because a teacher needed it, not because a developer thought it was clever.

"Collaborative language learning software built through conversation between a teacher who can't code and an AI who can."

The Result

FlashBoss runs on anything - even a potato computer. It works offline. It respects your time with focused daily sessions. And it turns vocabulary acquisition into something you actually want to do.

Six masters to guide you. Boss fights to challenge you. Real progress you can feel.

This is what happens when a language teacher builds exactly what they wish existed.
