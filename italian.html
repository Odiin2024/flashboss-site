Italian core design

From guest to family

Da ospite a famiglia.

Italian Core is one thousand words across five tiers, sequenced as a homecoming. The first three tiers are the Italy you visit — the piazza, the table, the market at eight in the morning. Survival, daily life, then the first real conversations and the past tense to tell them in. La dolce vita, taught straight.

Then it turns inward. The later tiers are the Italy you belong to — the imperfetto that lets you narrate your summers, the idioms nobody translates for an outsider, and the register that quietly sorts family from tourist.

Tiers 1–3 · Visit

Survival, daily life, getting around — then passato prossimo, reflexives, modals and clitic pronouns. The Italy you came for.

Tiers 4–5 · Belong

The tu/Lei register pivot, imperfetto, idiom, then future, conditional and the first subjunctive. The Italy you stay for.

Progress with FlashBoss

One sentence from each tier, in the order FlashBoss teaches them.

  1. Sono uno studente.I am a student.
  2. Ogni mattina compro il pane fresco al mercato vicino a casa.Every morning I buy fresh bread at the market near home.
  3. Ieri ho preso il treno delle nove per andare a trovare mia nonna.Yesterday I took the nine o'clock train to go and visit my grandmother.
  4. Da bambino, passavo le estati al mare con i miei cugini in Sicilia.As a child, I spent the summers by the sea with my cousins in Sicily.
  5. Spero che tu possa venire alla festa, anche se so che sei molto impegnato.I hope you can come to the party, even though I know you're very busy.
Read it by sight

il · la · l', in colour

Every noun carries its article colour-coded to gender. Tap a card to flip it.

blue — masculine (il, lo) rose — feminine (la)
il
libro
Il libro è sul tavolo.
the book — “The book is on the table.” Masculine, before a consonant.
tap to flip
la
casa
La casa di mia nonna è in Sicilia.
the house — “My grandmother's house is in Sicily.” Feminine, before a consonant.
tap to flip
l'
amico
L'amico di Marco abita a Roma.
the friend (m.) — “Marco's friend lives in Rome.” The article elides to l', but its colour stays blue: still masculine.
tap to flip
l'
amica
L'amica di Anna studia a Bologna.
the friend (f.) — “Anna's friend studies in Bologna.” Same l', but rose: feminine.
tap to flip

Italian hides gender the moment an article meets a vowel: lo amico and la amica both collapse to l'. FlashBoss keeps the cue alive by colouring the apostrophe — blue l' is masculine, rose l' is feminine — so the gender never disappears on you.

The words that let you in

Knowing these is the difference between a tourist and family. Tap to flip.

idiom
In bocca al lupo
— In bocca al lupo! — Crepi!
Good luck — literally “into the wolf's mouth.” You answer crepi (il lupo) — may the wolf die. Italians almost never say buona fortuna.
tap to flip
verb · concept
arrangiarsi
Non preoccuparti, mi arrangio.
To get by, to make do — “Don't worry, I'll manage.” The central Italian art of solving it yourself, with whatever you've got.
tap to flip
marker
magari
— Vieni in Italia quest'estate? — Magari!
If only / I wish / maybe — one word that carries hope and longing at once. “Magari!” on its own means “I wish!”
tap to flip
false friend
simpatico
Tuo cugino è molto simpatico.
Likeable, good company — not “sympathetic.” “Your cousin is great company.” High praise, and a classic false friend.
